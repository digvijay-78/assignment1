A CHRISTMAS CAROL — SCENE EXCERPT (Film Script Format)
SCENE 1 — INT. SCROOGE’S BEDROOM — NIGHT

The room is dim and silent. A single candle burns weakly.

Scrooge sits rigid in his chair, listening to the house settle.

A clock TICKS toward midnight.

SCROOGE
(whispering)
One o’clock… and nothing. I am not afraid. I am only… waiting.

A sudden DROP in temperature. The flame flickers violently.

Silence deepens.

SCENE 2 — INT. SCROOGE’S BEDROOM — CONTINUOUS

A warm, radiant LIGHT fills the room with no visible source.

A GLOWING FIGURE appears—youthful, bright, almost alive with movement.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
(booming, everywhere at once)
Come in! Come in! And know me better, man!

Scrooge rises slowly, uneasy but composed.

SCROOGE
I come with respect… for what you are.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
I am the Ghost of Christmas Present.

Scrooge studies him—awed despite himself.

SCROOGE
You seem… full of life itself.

The Ghost smiles warmly.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
I am young in time, yet I have more than eighteen hundred brothers.

A pause.

SCROOGE
Eighteen hundred…

The Ghost gestures gently.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
Touch my robe.

Scrooge hesitates… then reaches out.

SCROOGE
I will.

SCENE 3 — EXT. LONDON STREET — CHRISTMAS MORNING

The world TRANSFORMS in a burst of light and sound.

Crowded streets. Snow in the air. Warm chaos of life.

CROWD
(overlapping)
Merry Christmas!
Happy Christmas!
Bless you!

A SHOPKEEPER calls out from his stall.

SHOPKEEPER
Fresh apples! Oranges!

Children run past, laughing, throwing snow.

CHILDREN
Catch me!
Snowball!

Scrooge stands frozen in the middle of it all.

SCROOGE
(to Ghost)
Spirit… why such joy when there is so much suffering?

The Ghost’s tone sharpens, but remains calm.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
Because joy is still chosen, even where hardship exists.

Scrooge watches the crowd, unsettled.

SCROOGE
Do they not suffer?

The Ghost repeats his words back to him.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
Are there no prisons?
Are there no workhouses?

Scrooge stiffens.

SCROOGE
(quietly)
I said that… once.

SCENE 4 — EXT. LONDON STREET — CONTINUOUS

The Ghost’s robe begins to SHIFT.

Two thin, fragile CHILDREN emerge—dirty, frightened, clinging to him.

The sounds of the street fade.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
Behold them.

Scrooge recoils.

SCROOGE
What are they?

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
They are Man’s.
Ignorance.
And Want.

Silence now. No crowd. No sound.

Only them.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
Beware them both. But most of all—this boy.

Scrooge cannot look directly at them.

SCROOGE
Have they no help? No future?

The Ghost turns grave.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
If they persist, the boy becomes… doom.

Scrooge lowers his gaze, shaken.

SCENE 5 — INT. LIMINAL SPACE — NIGHT

The world begins to DISSOLVE.

Light fades. Sound collapses.

Scrooge stands alone again.

SCROOGE
Spirit… do not leave me.

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
My time on Earth is done. Midnight is near.

A distant BELL begins to toll.

SCROOGE
Show me more!

GHOST OF CHRISTMAS PRESENT
There is no more tonight.

The Ghost fades into light and disappears.

Silence.

Then—

A DARK FIGURE stands where the light was.

Tall. Hooded. Motionless.

Scrooge slowly turns.

SCROOGE
(whispering)
You are the next Spirit…

The figure does not move.

Only silence remains.

CUT TO BLACK.
