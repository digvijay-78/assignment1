Scene 1: Scrooge waits for the spirit
Scrooge wakes at one o’clock, waiting for the second spirit. He is nervous but determined not to be surprised.
Scene 2: The Ghost appears
Narration:
A strange voice calls Scrooge into the room. He obeys and enters.
Ghost:
“Come in! Come in! and know me better, man!”
Scrooge:
I come… but I do not know what I am meant to fear.”
Ghost:
“I am the Ghost of Christmas Present. Look upon me!”
Scene 3: Introduction of the Ghost
Narration:
Scrooge sees a bright, joyful spirit filled with warmth and light.
Scrooge:
“I have never seen the like of you before.”
Ghost:
“I am young… yet I have many brothers—more than eighteen hundred.”
Scrooge (quietly):
“A tremendous family…”

Ghost:
“Touch my robe!”
Scrooge:
“I will.”
Scene 4: Transformation to the streets

Narration:
The room vanishes, and Scrooge is taken to a busy city street on Christmas morning.

Crowd (together):
“Merry Christmas!”

Rooftop Worker 1:
“Watch out!”

Rooftop Worker 2:
“Take that snowball!”

(laughter)

Shopkeeper 2:
“Apples! Oranges! Finest in the city!”

Crowd:
“Hurry, let’s buy them!”

Scrooge Questions the Ghost

Scrooge:
“Spirit, why must people suffer like this? Don’t they deserve comfort?”

Spirit (firmly):
“They are not mine. They are Man’s responsibility.”

Scrooge:
“Have they no help?”

Spirit (echoing Scrooge’s old words):
“Are there no prisons? Are there no workhouses?”

(Scrooge looks ashamed)


Warning – Ignorance and Want

Narration:
The Ghost reveals two children hiding beneath his robe.

Spirit:
“Behold!”

Scrooge:
“What are they?”

Spirit:
“They are Ignorance and Want. Beware them both.”

Spirit (serious):
“Beware the boy most of all. His name is Doom unless mankind changes.”

Scrooge (softly):
“Oh, Spirit…”


SCENE 5:Ghost Fades Away

Narration:
The Ghost’s time is ending.

Scrooge:
“Are spirits’ lives so short?”

Spirit:
“My life ends at midnight.”

Scrooge (worried):
“Stay!”

Spirit:
“The time is near.”
Arrival of the Next Spirit

Narration:
The bell strikes twelve. The Ghost disappears.

A new figure appears—silent, dark, and hooded.

Scrooge (fearfully):
“Are you the next Spirit?”

(No reply – silence)
Narration:
Scrooge stands alone, facing the mysterious Ghost of the Future.
